Computer FAQ

How are computers used for teaching in the classroom?

Answer

Computers are used in assistance with class activities. They also assist with presentations.

Are there other tools used together with the computers?

Answer

There are projectors that help assist with presentations in class. They project the computer screen onto a blank surface.

How do computers enhance the learning experience?

Answer

Computers reduce the amount of resources for class activities, such as the use of paper printouts. It also allows for strong visual aid for presentations. Computers assist with digital activities and collaboration for group projects.

Computers can be vague. What other examples of computer electronic devices are used in the classroom other than desktops?

Answer

Other computer devices in the classroom consist of projectors and Smart Boards. There are video cameras that can also project information onto a blank surface similar to the projector. In many cases there are various pieces of small devices used for science classes, such as GPS systems and other devices that can be used for science activities.

The internet can be distracting. What are the restrictions for web browsing?

Answer

There is a firewall that blocks certain sites from being viewed. This restricts the students to school appropriate sites. Everything is secure on these computers to make sure the students aren't looking at unsuitable websites.


Scenarios

If students are listening to a presentation, how can they be more engaged with it?

Answer

While on the computer, students could be answering questions that go together with the presentation. This would be a simple participation exercise to see if they are listening.

If a student has a study hall, how would he or she work on homework using a computer?

Answer

Students are given access to computer via a login. They would be able to go to the library to work on an assignment that requires a computer.
